# Company Facing Active PR Crisis

## Situation Overview

A company is in the midst of an active public relations crisis. The triggering event may be a food safety incident, data breach, executive scandal, product failure, viral employee misconduct video, or regulatory enforcement action. The crisis is live — journalists are calling, social media is active with criticism, customers are expressing anger, and leadership is under pressure to respond.

Every hour without a competent response increases the damage. This is the highest-severity scenario because the situation evolves in real time and the window for effective intervention is narrow.

## Key Characteristics

- The event has attracted or is attracting mainstream media coverage.
- Social media is amplifying the story with criticism and potentially misinformation.
- Customers, partners, investors, or regulators are seeking information.
- The company may not yet have full facts about what happened.
- Internal communications may be chaotic, with employees uncertain about what to say.
- There is a risk of legal liability connected to the triggering event.

## Recommended Actions

### Hour 1-4: Immediate Containment

**Activate the crisis management plan** (see H15). If none exists, establish a crisis team with clear roles: spokesperson, decision-maker, legal adviser, communications lead, social media manager. Centralise all external communications through this team.

**Issue a holding statement.** Acknowledge the situation, express concern for those affected, state the matter is being investigated, and commit to providing updates. Avoid speculation, blame, or premature promises (see H16).

**Secure internal communications.** Brief employees and instruct them not to speak to media or post about the incident. Provide a clear contact point for enquiries.

### Hours 4-24: Active Management

**Gather facts rapidly.** The crisis team needs verified information quickly. Delayed responses carry greater risk than decisions made on incomplete information.

**Manage media actively** (see H16). Designate a single spokesperson. Prepare key messages and Q&A. Be proactive with updates rather than purely reactive.

**Monitor social media continuously** (see H17). Track sentiment, identify misinformation needing correction, respond to customer concerns. Do not delete legitimate criticism.

**Coordinate with legal counsel.** Review public statements for legal implications, but do not allow legal caution to create paralysing delays.

**Communicate with stakeholders directly.** Customers, partners, investors, and employees should receive tailored communications, not just generic public statements.

### Days 2-7: Sustained Response

**Provide regular updates** through press releases, social media, stakeholder communications, and the company website. **Demonstrate action** — announce concrete steps: recalls, policy changes, compensation, third-party investigations. **Monitor for secondary crises** that may spawn from employee leaks, regulatory investigations, or shareholder actions.

### Weeks 2-4: Transition to Recovery

Conduct a post-crisis review assessing what happened, what worked, what needs improvement, and what structural changes are needed. Begin long-term reputation recovery — see scenario S09 for the rebuild playbook.

Refer to case study CS11 for crisis management execution and the transition to recovery.

## Risks and Considerations

**Speed versus accuracy.** Acknowledge fast, provide detail carefully. The tension between the two is constant in a crisis.

**Legal exposure.** Crisis statements can be used in subsequent litigation. Coordinate with counsel without creating paralysis.

**Social media amplification.** A manageable crisis can escalate rapidly when thousands share and comment. Response speed must match criticism speed.

**Employee behaviour.** Uninformed or anxious employees may share speculation online. Proactive internal communication is essential.

**Misinformation.** False information spreads rapidly during crises. Correct significant misinformation without appearing defensive.

## Success Metrics

- Holding statement issued within four hours of crisis onset.
- Media coverage includes the company's perspective and key messages.
- Social media sentiment stabilises within 48-72 hours.
- Key stakeholders report feeling informed and engaged.
- No significant secondary crises from communication failures.
- Clear transition to post-crisis reputation recovery strategy.
