# New Client Onboarding and Assessment

## Situation Overview

A new client has approached the agency seeking reputation management services. They may have a specific problem, a general concern about their online presence, or a proactive desire to build their digital reputation. Regardless of the entry point, every engagement begins with a structured onboarding and assessment process that establishes the facts, defines scope, and sets realistic expectations.

This scenario covers the initial phase of any engagement — from first contact through to strategy recommendation and agreement. A thorough assessment ensures the right services are recommended, accurate timelines and budgets are set, and the relationship starts on a foundation of trust.

## Key Characteristics

- The client may be an individual or a company of any size.
- They may arrive with a specific urgent issue or a general desire to improve their presence.
- They may have unrealistic expectations about achievability, speed, or cost.
- They may have attempted DIY reputation management with limited success.
- They may be emotionally distressed, particularly in cases involving personal attacks or criminal records.

## Recommended Actions

### Stage 1: Initial Fact-Finding (Meeting 1)

The first conversation should focus on comprehensive understanding (see H28):

- **Specific concern.** Identify the URLs, search terms, platforms, and content types causing problems.
- **Context.** When did the issue arise, what caused it, are there ongoing legal or regulatory matters?
- **Impact.** How is the issue affecting the client — lost business, failed applications, personal distress?
- **Prior efforts.** What has already been tried? This avoids duplication and identifies what has not worked.
- **Goals.** What does success look like — complete removal, improved search results, stronger presence, crisis prevention?
- **Constraints.** Budget, timeline, legal considerations, internal resources, regulatory obligations.

### Stage 2: Reputation Audit (Week 1-2)

Conduct a comprehensive audit (see H27, H28) covering:

- **SERP analysis.** Document every result on pages one through three for the client's name or brand, noting sentiment, source, domain authority, and content type.
- **Social media audit.** Review owned accounts, mentions, tagged content, and third-party commentary across platforms.
- **Review platform audit.** Check Google Business Profile, Trustpilot, Glassdoor, and industry-specific sites. Document ratings, trends, and flagged or fake reviews.
- **Content gap analysis.** Identify where the positive content footprint is weak — missing profiles, under-optimised website, unexploited content opportunities.

### Stage 3: Strategy Recommendation (Week 2-3)

Develop a tailored strategy recommendation including:

- **Prioritised actions.** Sequence by urgency and impact — removal requests, crisis response, content creation, review management.
- **Realistic timelines.** Removal may take weeks to months. Suppression typically requires three to six months. Brand building is ongoing.
- **Budget and resources.** Transparent cost breakdown by activity so the client can make informed scope decisions.
- **Success metrics.** Measurable outcomes both parties can track — SERP positions, review ratings, content volume, sentiment scores.
- **Risk assessment.** Potential obstacles and how they will be managed.

### Stage 4: Agreement and Kickoff (Week 3-4)

Formalise the engagement with a clear scope of work, timeline, budget, and reporting schedule. Establish communication protocols and set up monitoring tools, content calendars, and reporting dashboards.

## Risks and Considerations

**Over-promising.** The most common onboarding mistake is setting expectations too high. Honest expectation-setting protects the relationship long-term.

**Emotional clients.** Clients in distress may push for immediate action before assessment is complete. Empathy is essential, but rushing can lead to wasted effort or counterproductive steps.

**Scope creep.** Clearly define inclusions and exclusions. Reputation projects expand rapidly if boundaries are not established early.

**Conflict checks.** Ensure no conflicts of interest — the agency should not manage reputation for two parties in a dispute.

## Success Metrics

- Comprehensive audit completed and presented within agreed timeframe.
- Client has clear understanding of their situation, recommended approach, and realistic expected outcomes.
- Engagement agreement signed with defined scope, timeline, budget, and reporting cadence.
- Onboarding infrastructure in place before active work begins.
