# Reputation Audit and Monitoring Setup

## Situation Overview

A client needs a comprehensive audit of their current online reputation and wants to establish an ongoing monitoring system to detect changes, threats, and opportunities in real time. This may arise as a standalone engagement or as the foundational phase of a broader reputation management programme.

The audit provides a baseline understanding of the client's reputation across search engines, social media, review platforms, and the wider web. The monitoring system then tracks changes against this baseline, alerting the team to new negative content, review rating shifts, media mentions, and SERP position changes before they become established problems.

## Key Characteristics

- The client may or may not have an existing reputation issue — the audit will reveal the current state.
- No comprehensive mapping of the client's online presence has been done previously.
- The client wants data-driven insight, not assumptions or anecdotes.
- There is a need for ongoing vigilance, particularly in competitive, regulated, or public-facing industries.
- Multiple entities may require monitoring — corporate brand, sub-brands, key executives, product names.

## Recommended Actions

### Stage 1: SERP Audit (Week 1)

Conduct a detailed search engine results audit (see H27, H28) for every relevant search term: brand name, common misspellings, brand combined with key modifiers ("reviews", "complaints", "scam"), executive names, and product names. For each term, document every result on pages one through three, recording URL, title, snippet, sentiment, content type, domain authority, and date.

### Stage 2: Social Media Audit (Week 1-2)

Audit the client's social media presence across all relevant platforms. Assess owned profile completeness, branding consistency, engagement metrics, posting frequency, sentiment in comments and mentions, third-party commentary, and competitive benchmarking.

### Stage 3: Review Platform Audit (Week 1-2)

Audit Google Business Profile, Trustpilot, Glassdoor, TripAdvisor, and industry-specific review sites (see H27). Document overall ratings, review volume, recent trends, response rates, and any suspicious patterns indicating fake reviews.

### Stage 4: Monitoring System Setup (Week 2-3)

Design and implement an ongoing monitoring system with these components:

- **SERP monitoring.** Weekly position tracking for priority search terms, with alerts on significant rank changes or new negative results entering page one.
- **Social media monitoring.** Social listening tools tracking brand mentions, sentiment trends, and conversation spikes.
- **Review monitoring.** Real-time tracking of new reviews across all platforms, with alerts for reviews below a defined rating threshold.
- **News and web monitoring.** Google Alerts and supplementary tools to detect new content — news articles, blog posts, forum discussions.

### Stage 5: Reporting Framework (Week 3-4)

Establish a regular reporting cadence including a SERP scorecard, review dashboard, social media summary, alert log, and actionable recommendations. Reports should include analysis and interpretation, not just raw metrics.

Refer to case studies CS06 and CS09 for examples of how monitoring enabled early detection and rapid response to emerging threats.

## Risks and Considerations

**Tool selection.** Select monitoring tools that match the client's scale. Enterprise clients need enterprise-grade solutions; smaller clients can often be served by Google Alerts, platform notifications, and targeted SERP tracking.

**Alert fatigue.** Set thresholds carefully to ensure the team is notified of genuinely significant events without being overwhelmed by noise.

**Data interpretation.** A spike in negative mentions might indicate a crisis or simply a viral post that will pass. Reports should include expert analysis, not just metrics.

**Ongoing commitment.** Monitoring is only valuable if someone acts on the insights. Ensure clear processes for reviewing data and escalating significant findings.

## Success Metrics

- Comprehensive baseline audit completed and documented.
- Monitoring system operational across all priority channels.
- Regular reporting cadence established and maintained.
- First actionable insight identified and acted upon within the initial monitoring period.
- Client has a clear, data-driven understanding of their reputation landscape.
