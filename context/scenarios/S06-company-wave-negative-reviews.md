# Company Hit by Wave of Negative Reviews

## Situation Overview

A company is experiencing a sudden spike in negative reviews across one or more platforms — Google Business Profile, Trustpilot, Glassdoor, TripAdvisor, or social media. The reviews may stem from a genuine service failure, a disgruntled former employee campaign, competitor sabotage, or a viral social media incident motivating a pile-on from non-customers.

The immediate effect is a visible drop in star ratings, a wall of negative recent reviews, and potential loss of customer trust. For businesses dependent on reviews for lead generation — hospitality, healthcare, legal services, e-commerce — the commercial impact can be severe and rapid.

## Key Characteristics

- A noticeable spike in negative reviews over days to weeks, breaking from the normal pattern.
- Reviews may appear across multiple platforms simultaneously.
- Some reviews may be genuine; others may be fake, coordinated, or from non-customers.
- The company's overall star rating has dropped noticeably.
- Negative reviews appear in Google search results via review snippets in the knowledge panel.
- Staff morale may be affected, particularly if Glassdoor is involved.

## Recommended Actions

### Immediate Response (Days 1-3)

**Investigate the root cause.** Is there a genuine operational issue? Has an incident gone viral? Is there evidence of a coordinated fake campaign? The root cause determines the strategy.

**Set up monitoring** (see H27). Track new reviews across all platforms in real time.

**Do not panic-respond.** Avoid defensive, emotional, or template responses. Each response should be considered, professional, and tailored.

### Short-Term Response (Weeks 1-4)

**Respond to every review** following best practices (see H06, H26). For genuine complaints, acknowledge, apologise where appropriate, and offer offline resolution. For fake or policy-violating reviews, flag them for removal through platform reporting mechanisms.

**Address the root cause.** If reviews reflect a genuine issue, fix it and communicate the fix publicly — visible response to criticism demonstrates commitment to improvement.

**Report fake reviews.** Document evidence thoroughly: accounts with no history, reviews posted in clusters, inaccurate references, or suspiciously similar language (see H06).

### Medium-Term Recovery (Months 1-3)

**Implement a positive review strategy** (see H26). Ethically encourage satisfied customers to leave reviews through post-purchase emails, in-store prompts, and follow-up after positive interactions. Never incentivise, buy, or fabricate reviews.

**Conduct a broader reputation audit** (see H27) to understand how the review crisis has affected overall online presence and search results.

### Long-Term Strategy (Months 3-12)

Build a sustainable review generation process integrated into standard business operations. Maintain ongoing monitoring with alerts for rating changes and review volume spikes.

Refer to case study CS04 for a company that successfully recovered from a major negative review spike.

## Risks and Considerations

**Platform limitations.** Platforms are often slow to remove reported reviews and may not cover all types of unfair content. Some negative reviews will remain.

**Legal risks of responding.** Public responses can create exposure if they disclose confidential customer information. Establish response protocols and involve legal counsel for sensitive cases.

**Employee review platforms.** Glassdoor responses are constrained by employment law and confidentiality. Responses must avoid identifying individuals while addressing substance.

## Success Metrics

- Average star rating stabilised and trending upward.
- Fake or policy-violating reviews successfully removed.
- Positive review volume increased through ethical strategies.
- Negative review content no longer dominating search results or knowledge panels.
- Root cause identified and addressed to prevent recurrence.
