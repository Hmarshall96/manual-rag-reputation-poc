# Negative Results Dominating Brand SERP

## Situation Overview

Multiple negative results are appearing on page one and page two of Google when the brand name (or a key individual's name) is searched. Unlike a single problematic article, this involves a pattern of negative content across several sources — news articles, review sites, forum posts, complaint sites, social media threads, or regulatory records. The cumulative effect is an overwhelmingly negative first impression for anyone searching the brand.

This is one of the most challenging scenarios because it requires a multi-pronged approach combining removal, suppression, and positive content creation over several months.

## Key Characteristics

- Three or more negative results appear on Google page one for the brand or individual name.
- Negative content comes from multiple source types, not just one outlet.
- The brand's own website may be outranked by negative third-party content.
- The brand has a weak positive content footprint, leaving space for negative results to dominate.
- Commercial impact is significant — potential customers, partners, and investors encounter negativity during routine searches.

## Recommended Actions

### Phase 1: Comprehensive SERP Audit (Week 1)

Audit all results on pages one through three for every relevant search term. For each result, document URL, domain authority, content type, removability assessment, and specific negative claims. This audit is the foundation of the entire strategy.

### Phase 2: Removal and De-listing (Weeks 2-8)

Assess the most viable removal path for each negative result (see A01):

- **RTBF de-listing** (see H01) for outdated content about individuals in the UK/EU.
- **Legal removal** for defamatory or factually inaccurate content.
- **Platform policy removal** for content violating terms of service.
- **Direct negotiation** with publishers for corrections or removal.

Prioritise by damage severity and likelihood of success. Accept that some results must be suppressed rather than removed.

### Phase 3: Content Suppression (Weeks 2-16)

For non-removable results, implement systematic suppression (see A02, H22, H23, H24, H25):

- **Own the branded SERP.** Fully optimise the brand's website, social profiles, and digital properties (see H22, H25).
- **Create high-authority content.** Publish articles, press releases, and thought leadership on platforms with strong domain authority (see H18, H24).
- **Build profile pages.** Claim and optimise listings on industry directories and professional networks.
- **Leverage social media.** Active, optimised social profiles rank well for brand names (see H23).

Refer to case studies CS01 (CCI Global), CS03 (US Tech CEO), and CS07 for successful multi-result suppression campaigns.

### Phase 4: Ongoing Monitoring (Months 4-12+)

Suppression is not permanent. Implement monitoring (see A05, A08) that tracks SERP positions weekly, alerts on new negative content, and triggers content refreshes when needed.

## Risks and Considerations

**Timeline expectations.** Three to six months for meaningful page-one improvement; six to twelve months for a comprehensively positive SERP. Set expectations from the outset.

**Content quality.** Suppression content must be genuinely valuable. Search engines penalise thin or manufactured content, and transparent manipulation backfires.

**New negative content.** Competitor activity or media coverage can appear at any time, potentially undoing progress. Robust monitoring provides early warning.

**Resource requirements.** This scenario typically requires the most sustained investment of any reputation engagement. Ensure the client understands the commitment.

## Success Metrics

- Negative results on page one reduced from baseline to two or fewer.
- Brand-owned and positive content occupies the majority of page one.
- Branded search click-through rates improve as negative snippets are displaced.
- Monitoring shows stable or improving SERP composition over time.
