# Wikipedia Page Management

## Situation Overview

A client needs assistance with a Wikipedia page — creating a new page, editing an existing page to correct inaccuracies, or managing damage from hostile edits. Wikipedia articles frequently rank on page one of Google for personal and brand names, making them among the most visible pieces of online content for reputation purposes.

Wikipedia operates under strict editorial policies that make it fundamentally different from other platforms. It is governed by volunteer editors who enforce neutrality, verifiability, notability, and conflict of interest policies. Any reputation management activity that violates these policies risks being reverted, flagged, or publicly exposed, potentially causing greater damage than the original problem.

## Key Characteristics

- A Wikipedia article about the client contains inaccurate, outdated, or negatively weighted content — or no article exists and the client wants one created.
- The article ranks on page one of Google, amplifying any content issues.
- The client or their representatives may have previously attempted direct edits, triggering conflict-of-interest flags.
- Hostile editors may be actively maintaining negative content or reverting positive changes.

## Recommended Actions

### Stage 1: Policy Education (Week 1)

Ensure the client understands Wikipedia's core policies:

- **Neutral Point of View (NPOV).** Articles must represent all significant viewpoints fairly. Content resembling a press release will be flagged and reverted.
- **Verifiability.** Every claim must be supported by citations from reliable, independent secondary sources. The client's own website and press releases do not qualify.
- **Notability.** Subjects must have received significant coverage in reliable, independent sources. Not everyone qualifies for a page.
- **Conflict of interest (COI).** Wikipedia strongly discourages editing articles about yourself. Paid editing must be disclosed under the Wikimedia Foundation's Terms of Use. Undisclosed paid editing can result in blocks and public exposure.

### Stage 2: Assessment (Week 1-2)

**For existing articles:** Review content, edit history, talk page, and ongoing disputes. Identify inaccuracies, outdated information, or policy violations (unsourced claims, undue weight on negative events). Document issues with specific Wikipedia policy citations.

**For new articles:** Assess whether the client meets notability guidelines. Search for independent, reliable secondary sources. If insufficient sources exist, the page will be nominated for deletion, often within hours.

### Stage 3: Source Development (Weeks 2-8)

Wikipedia content is only as strong as its sources (see H18). Secure genuine editorial media coverage in recognised outlets — press releases do not count. Research existing coverage not yet cited. Compile a source library with full citations noting which claims each source supports.

### Stage 4: Editorial Engagement (Weeks 4-12)

**Never edit the article directly if paid by the subject.** Instead:

- **Use the talk page.** Raise concerns about inaccuracies on the article's talk page. Present evidence calmly, cite specific policies, and propose changes.
- **Request edits through proper channels.** Use the "Edit Request" template for protected articles.
- **Engage independent editors.** Experienced Wikipedia editors can evaluate the article independently and make changes they believe are warranted — but they must exercise genuine editorial judgement.
- **File dispute resolution requests.** If hostile editors persist with unsourced or biased content, use Wikipedia's dispute resolution processes.

### Stage 5: Monitoring (Ongoing)

Set up alerts for changes using Wikipedia's watchlist or third-party tools. Respond to hostile edits through talk page discussion rather than edit wars.

## Risks and Considerations

**Disclosure requirements.** Wikimedia's Terms of Use require paid contributors to disclose their affiliation. Several reputation firms have been publicly named for undisclosed paid editing.

**Edit wars.** Repeatedly reverting other editors' changes can result in article protection and editor blocks. Always use talk page discussion.

**No guaranteed outcome.** Wikipedia's processes are community-driven. Even well-sourced proposals may not be accepted if editors disagree.

**Deletion risk.** Articles that do not meet notability standards are frequently nominated for deletion. A failed deletion discussion creates a public record that is itself reputationally damaging.

## Success Metrics

- Inaccurate or unsourced negative content corrected through policy-compliant processes.
- Article reflects a balanced, well-sourced representation of the client.
- No conflict-of-interest violations or paid editing exposure incidents.
- Monitoring system in place to detect and respond to future hostile edits.
