# How to Remove a Google Business Review or Listing

## Overview

Negative Google reviews and outdated Google Business listings can cause significant reputational damage. A single one-star review at the top of search results can deter potential customers, while an old or inaccurate business listing can create confusion and attract unwanted attention. This guide covers two distinct processes: removing an entire Google Business listing and reporting or removing individual reviews that violate Google's policies.

## Part 1: Removing a Google Business Listing

### When to Remove a Listing

Removing a Google Business listing is appropriate when:

- The business has permanently closed
- The listing is for a business that no longer exists at the listed location
- The listing is attracting negative reviews for a business that has ceased trading
- The listing is inaccurate and cannot be corrected through standard editing
- An old listing is causing reputational damage to the individual formerly associated with the business

### Method: Suggest an Edit

Google allows anyone to suggest edits to a business listing, including suggesting that a place no longer exists. This is the primary method for removing a listing.

**Step-by-step process:**

1. Search for the business on Google Maps or Google Search
2. Click on the business listing to open its full profile
3. Click the **three dots** (more options menu) located near the number of reviews or the business name
4. Click **"Suggest an edit"**
5. Click **"Place is closed or not here"**
6. Select the most applicable option. The most viable option for removal is **"Doesn't exist here"**
7. Submit the suggestion

**Processing time:** Google reviews suggested edits and typically processes them within 2-4 weeks. You will not receive a notification of the outcome -- you need to check back manually.

### Handling Active Listings

If the business listing has had recent activity (recent reviews, recent check-ins, recent photos uploaded by users), Google is less likely to accept a "Doesn't exist here" suggestion because the activity suggests the place is still operational.

In this case, use the following two-stage approach:

**Stage 1: Permanently Close the Business**

1. If you have access to the Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) account, log in and mark the business as "Permanently closed"
2. If you do not have access, suggest an edit (using the three-dot menu method above) and select "Permanently closed" rather than "Doesn't exist here"
3. Wait approximately **one month** for the "Permanently closed" status to take effect and for activity to die down

**Stage 2: Request Full Removal**

1. After approximately one month, return to the listing
2. Click the three dots, then "Suggest an edit"
3. Click "Place is closed or not here"
4. Now select **"Doesn't exist here"**
5. Submit the suggestion

The two-stage approach has a much higher success rate because Google is more willing to remove a listing that has already been marked as permanently closed and has had no recent activity.

### Verification and Follow-Up

After submitting a removal suggestion:

- Check the listing weekly for 4 weeks
- If the listing is still present after 4 weeks, resubmit the suggestion
- If repeated suggestions are ignored, consider escalating through Google Business Profile support (requires an active Google Business Profile account)
- As a last resort, use Google's legal removal tools if the listing contains defamatory content

## Part 2: Removing Individual Google Reviews

### When You Can Report a Review

Google will remove reviews that violate its policies. Reviewable violations include:

- **Spam and fake content**: Reviews that are not based on a real experience, or posted by bots
- **Off-topic content**: Reviews that do not relate to the actual experience at the business
- **Restricted content**: Reviews promoting regulated goods or services
- **Illegal content**: Reviews that describe or promote illegal activity
- **Sexually explicit content**: Inappropriate sexual material
- **Offensive content**: Content containing hate speech, harassment, or threats
- **Impersonation**: Reviews posted by someone pretending to be someone else
- **Conflict of interest**: Reviews posted by current or former employees, competitors, or other parties with a direct interest

### How to Report a Review

**Method 1: Through Google Maps**

1. Find the business listing on Google Maps
2. Scroll down to the Reviews section
3. Find the specific review you want to report
4. Click the **three dots** next to the review
5. Click **"Flag as inappropriate"** or **"Report review"**
6. Select the reason that best describes the policy violation
7. Submit the report

**Method 2: Through Google Business Profile**

If you manage the business listing through Google Business Profile:

1. Log in to your Google Business Profile account
2. Navigate to the Reviews section
3. Find the review in question
4. Click the three-dot menu next to the review
5. Select **"Report review"**
6. Choose the applicable policy violation
7. Submit

**Method 3: Through Google's Legal Removal Tools**

For reviews that are defamatory (not just negative, but contain false statements of fact that damage reputation), you can submit a legal removal request:

1. Go to Google's legal help page for removing content
2. Select the option for removing content from Google
3. Follow the prompts to submit a legal removal request
4. Provide the specific URL of the review, the defamatory content, and an explanation of why it is defamatory
5. Include any supporting evidence (court orders, solicitor's letters, etc.)

### Processing Times

| Method | Typical Processing Time |
|---|---|
| Flag as inappropriate (Maps) | 1-3 weeks |
| Report via Business Profile | 1-3 weeks |
| Legal removal request | 2-4 weeks |
| Escalation via Business Profile support | 1-2 weeks additional |

### What to Do If Google Declines to Remove

If Google declines to remove a review after your initial report, you have several options:

**1. Re-report with more detail**

If the initial report was brief, resubmit with a more detailed explanation of exactly which policy the review violates. Quote specific language from the review and map it to specific Google policies.

**2. Escalate through Google Business Profile support**

If you have an active Google Business Profile account, use the support channel to escalate. This gives you access to a human reviewer rather than relying on automated systems.

**3. Submit a legal removal request**

If the review contains defamatory content, escalate to Google's legal removal tools. A legal removal request receives different treatment from a standard policy violation report.

**4. Respond to the review**

If removal is not possible, post a professional, measured response to the review. This does not remove the negative review but provides context for other users reading it. Guidelines for responding:

- Remain calm and professional
- Acknowledge the reviewer's experience (even if you dispute the facts)
- Provide your version of events briefly
- Offer to resolve the issue offline
- Do not get into a public argument

**5. Bury the review with positive reviews**

Encourage satisfied customers to leave genuine, positive reviews. A single negative review among twenty positive ones has far less impact than a negative review that stands alone. Never purchase fake reviews -- this violates Google's policies and can result in penalties.

## Part 3: Preventing Future Problems

### Proactive Listing Management

- **Claim your Google Business Profile**: If you have not already, claim and verify your business listing. This gives you control over the information displayed and access to management tools.
- **Keep information current**: Regularly update business hours, contact details, and photos. An active, well-maintained listing looks more professional.
- **Monitor reviews**: Set up alerts for new reviews so you can respond quickly to negative feedback before it escalates.
- **Respond to all reviews**: Not just negative ones. Responding to positive reviews shows engagement and builds a track record of customer interaction.

### When a Listing Should Not Exist

If you discover a Google Business listing has been created for an entity that should not have one (e.g., a department within a company that should not be listed separately, a personal name listed as a business, or a defunct entity), act promptly:

1. Suggest its removal using the "Doesn't exist here" method described above
2. If you have a Google Business Profile account, report the listing as a duplicate or as an entity that should not be listed
3. If the listing is attracting targeted negative reviews, escalate through both the standard and legal removal channels simultaneously

## Timeline Summary

| Action | Timeline |
|---|---|
| Report individual review | 1-3 weeks for Google to process |
| Suggest listing edit/removal | 2-4 weeks for Google to process |
| Two-stage removal (close then remove) | ~1 month wait + 2-4 weeks processing |
| Legal removal request | 2-4 weeks for Google to process |
| Positive review campaign | Ongoing; visible impact in 1-3 months |

## Summary

Google review and listing removal requires patience and a methodical approach. For listings, the "Suggest an edit" pathway with the "Doesn't exist here" option is the primary tool, with a two-stage approach (permanently close first, then request removal) for listings with recent activity. For individual reviews, report through Google Maps, Google Business Profile, or Google's legal tools depending on the nature of the violation. When removal is not possible, professional responses and positive review generation are effective mitigation strategies. Throughout the process, expect timelines of 2-4 weeks per action and plan accordingly.
