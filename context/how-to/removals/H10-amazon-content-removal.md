# How to Request Amazon Content Removal

Amazon hosts a significant volume of user-generated content including product reviews, book reviews, seller feedback, and author profiles. Defamatory content on Amazon can be particularly damaging because of the platform's high domain authority and the trust consumers place in its review systems. This guide covers the formal process for requesting removal of defamatory content from Amazon UK, including the specific requirements and practical steps.

## Types of Content That Can Be Reported

Defamatory content on Amazon can appear in several places:

- **Product reviews**: customer reviews of products you sell or are associated with
- **Book reviews**: reviews of books you have authored or are the subject of
- **Books and publications**: content within books available on Amazon (Kindle or physical) that contains defamatory statements about you
- **Product listings**: product descriptions or associated content that contains defamatory claims
- **Seller feedback**: feedback left for your seller account
- **Q&A sections**: questions and answers on product pages

Each of these can be addressed through Amazon's defamation notice process, though the specific details you need to provide differ depending on where the content appears.

## The Formal Defamation Notice Process

Amazon UK provides a dedicated channel for defamation complaints. This is the most effective route for content that is genuinely defamatory rather than merely negative.

### Contact Details

- **Email**: defamation-notice@amazon.co.uk
- **Subject line**: UK Defamation Notice -- [your full name]
- **Online form**: https://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/help/customer/display.html?nodeId=TWGNOPYgM2d4Zbcbxv

The online form mirrors the email requirements, so you can use whichever method you prefer. The email route allows you to attach supporting documents more easily.

### Required Information in Your Notice

Amazon requires a structured notice that includes all of the following. Incomplete notices will be rejected or delayed, so ensure you address every point before submitting.

#### 1. Personal Details

You must provide:
- **Full name**: your legal name as it appears on official documents
- **Postal address**: your current residential or business address
- **Telephone number**: a number where you can be contacted
- **Email address**: preferably the same email address associated with any Amazon account you hold
- **Occupation**: your current professional role
- **Company details**: if the defamation relates to your business, provide the company name, registered address, and company number where applicable

#### 2. Location of Defamatory Content

You must specify exactly where the defamatory content appears. Amazon requires you to identify the location using one of the following formats:

- **(a) On a website page**: provide the full URL of the page where the defamatory content appears. Navigate to the specific review, product page, or listing and copy the URL from your browser.
- **(b) In a book**: provide the book title, author name, ISBN number, and the specific page numbers where defamatory content appears. For Kindle editions, provide the location numbers.
- **(c) In a product listing**: provide the ASIN (Amazon Standard Identification Number) for the product. The ASIN is a 10-character alphanumeric code found in the product details section or in the URL of the product page.

#### 3. The Defamatory Content

You must quote the exact defamatory content word for word. Do not summarise or paraphrase. Copy the precise text from the review, book, or listing. If there are multiple defamatory passages, quote each one separately.

#### 4. Explanation of Why the Content Is Defamatory

For each quoted passage, explain:
- **Why it is defamatory**: what false factual impression does it create? How does it damage your reputation?
- **Why it is defamatory of you**: how does the content identify you, either directly by name or by implication?
- **Why it is unlawful**: reference the relevant legal framework. For UK-based notices, this is typically the Defamation Act 2013, which requires that a statement causes or is likely to cause serious harm to reputation. You may also reference UK GDPR if personal data is being processed unlawfully.

Be specific and factual. General claims that content is "unfair" or "hurtful" are insufficient. You need to demonstrate that the content contains false statements of fact that damage your reputation.

#### 5. Confirmation Regarding the Original Poster

You must confirm that you do not have sufficient information to identify the person who originally posted the defamatory content. This is a standard requirement because Amazon's process is designed for situations where the complainant cannot pursue the matter directly with the poster.

If you do know who posted the content, Amazon may direct you to pursue the matter with that individual first before they will intervene.

#### 6. Consent Regarding Information Sharing

Amazon will ask whether you agree or disagree to your name and email address being shared with the person who posted the content. Consider this carefully:

- **Agreeing** allows the poster to know who has complained, which may facilitate resolution but also means you lose anonymity in the complaint.
- **Disagreeing** keeps your identity confidential from the poster, but Amazon may have more limited options for mediating a resolution.

In most cases, agreeing to share your details demonstrates confidence in your complaint and may expedite the process.

## What Happens After Submission

Once Amazon receives your defamation notice:

1. **Acknowledgement**: you should receive an automated acknowledgement of receipt. If you do not receive one within a few business days, follow up.
2. **Review**: Amazon's legal or trust and safety team will review your notice against their policies and the applicable law.
3. **Action**: if Amazon agrees the content is defamatory, they may remove the review, listing, or content. They may also contact the poster to give them an opportunity to respond.
4. **Notification**: you will typically receive notification of the outcome, though Amazon does not always provide detailed reasoning.

Response times vary. Simple cases involving clearly defamatory reviews may be resolved within one to two weeks. More complex cases involving books or multiple pieces of content may take longer.

## Alternative Approaches

If the formal defamation notice process does not achieve the desired result, consider these alternatives:

### Amazon Community Guidelines Violation

Amazon has community guidelines that prohibit content including harassment, threats, and content that is not relevant to the product. You can report content that violates these guidelines through the "Report abuse" button available on most reviews and Q&A sections. This is faster than the defamation notice route but covers a narrower range of issues.

### Amazon Brand Registry

If you are a registered brand owner, Amazon's Brand Registry programme provides additional tools for managing content associated with your products, including the ability to report inaccurate product information and intellectual property violations.

### Contact the Reviewer

In some cases, particularly for product reviews that are based on a misunderstanding or outdated experience, reaching out to the reviewer through Amazon's buyer-seller messaging system can lead to a voluntary edit or removal. This only works if you are the seller and the reviewer is identifiable through your order history.

### Legal Action

If Amazon declines to remove content and the defamation is causing serious harm, you may need to pursue legal action. This could include obtaining a court order requiring Amazon to remove the content or to disclose the identity of an anonymous poster so that you can pursue defamation proceedings against them directly. This is a costly and time-consuming route and should be considered only after other options have been exhausted.

## Practical Tips

- **Act quickly**: the longer defamatory content remains on Amazon, the more damage it causes and the more likely it is to be cached by search engines and third-party sites.
- **Screenshot everything**: before submitting your notice, take timestamped screenshots of all defamatory content. Content may be edited or removed by the poster at any time, and you need a record.
- **Be precise**: vague or overly broad notices are more likely to be rejected. Focus on specific, clearly defamatory statements of fact.
- **Keep records**: save all correspondence with Amazon, including submission confirmations, reference numbers, and responses.
- **Monitor after removal**: even if content is removed from Amazon, it may persist in Google's cache or on third-party review aggregation sites. Use Google's Outdated Content Tool to address cached versions and monitor for content reappearing.

Amazon's defamation notice process is a structured and effective route for removing genuinely defamatory content. Success depends on providing a complete, well-evidenced notice that clearly demonstrates why the content meets the legal threshold for defamation.
