# The Symphony Group - Online Review Management

## Context

The Symphony Group, a UK-wide manufacturing business with multiple corporate customers, was struggling to respond to and resolve negative reviews in search results. The negative reviews largely concerned matters out of their control — issues with third-party customers. The company lacked internal resources to manage customer communications and reviews across multiple review platforms.

## Issue Type

- Negative reviews across multiple platforms
- Poor response rates and review management
- Low Trustpilot rating (5.4 stars — "poor")
- No structured process for intercepting unhappy customers before they posted online

## Actions Taken

1. **Online Review Audit**: Analysed current state across all review sites — volumes, sentiment, and star ratings to establish benchmarks
2. **Team Training**: Worked with Communications Team and regional stakeholders to advise on best practice for review responses
3. **Daily Monitoring & Response**: Managed daily monitoring and response of reviews across platforms
4. **Process Improvement**: Identified gaps in customer care process — found opportunity to intercept unhappy customers before they went online
5. **Customer Portal**: Created a new online portal for the public to present customer service information, reducing the volume of negative reviews and complaints

## Outcome

- Working relationship sustained for over 2 years
- Average review rating up 6.35%
- Total reviews up 127%
- Response rate up 121%
- Trustpilot score improved from 5.4 stars (poor) to 7.0 stars (great)

## Lessons Learned

- Review management is an ongoing discipline, not a one-off fix — the 2-year relationship shows sustained effort is required
- Intercepting unhappy customers before they post online is more effective than responding after the fact
- When a company's negative reviews stem from third-party issues, a customer information portal can redirect complaints to the right channels
- Training internal teams on response best practices multiplies the effect — it builds internal capability rather than dependency
- Increasing total review volume (up 127%) dilutes the impact of existing negative reviews — encouraging positive reviews is as important as managing negative ones
- Daily monitoring ensures no review goes unanswered, which signals responsiveness to prospective customers
