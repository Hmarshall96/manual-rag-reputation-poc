# Coral Sea Resorts - Food Poisoning Crisis Management and Recovery

## Context

Coral Sea Resorts in Egypt experienced a severe food poisoning outbreak that had a devastating impact on the company's online reputation. Bookings dropped drastically. The company needed both immediate crisis management and a long-term reputation recovery strategy.

## Issue Type

- Severe food poisoning outbreak at resort
- Devastating online reputation damage
- Drastically dropped bookings
- Negative online sentiment dominating brand conversations
- Dual need: immediate crisis containment + long-term recovery

## Actions Taken

### Immediate Crisis Response
1. **On-Site Audit**: Visited resort sites to audit the impact of the situation
2. **Crisis Management Plan**: Created guidelines for communicating with media in every possible scenario related to the crisis, including tools, checklists, immediate steps, and roles/responsibilities

### Reputation Recovery (12-month plan)
3. **Full Online Reputation Audit**: Analysed all online comments and sentiment — who was saying what, where, and what reach it had
4. **Content Creation**: Focused on creating positive new content about the resorts
5. **Social Media Management**: Engaged users on social media to turn the tide of conversation
6. **SEO**: Optimized content and channels for search visibility

## Outcome

- Crisis was well handled and calm was restored online
- Significant social media growth over 12 months:
  - Facebook page likes: +352.5%
  - Twitter followers: +345%
  - YouTube video views: +95.9%
  - Instagram followers: +383.3%
- New visitors attracted to company-owned content and channels
- The incident did not permanently tar the resorts' reputation

## Lessons Learned

- Crisis management has two distinct phases: immediate containment (media guidelines, roles, checklists) and long-term recovery (content, social, SEO) — both must be planned from day one
- An on-site audit is critical for crisis situations — understanding the physical reality informs the communication strategy
- Media communication guidelines must cover every possible scenario, not just the most likely — journalists will find the angle you didn't prepare for
- Social media is the fastest channel for reputation recovery — the 300%+ growth rates show that audiences will engage with positive content if it's consistent and authentic
- A 12-month recovery timeline is realistic for severe crises — shorter commitments risk regression
- Structure and best practice guidance (checklists, roles, responsibilities) are essential in a crisis because the situation is emotionally charged and decisions must be made quickly
- "Turning the tide of conversation" requires volume — you cannot out-debate negative sentiment, you must out-produce it with positive content
