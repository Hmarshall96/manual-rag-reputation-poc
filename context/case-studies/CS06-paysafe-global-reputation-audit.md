# Paysafe - Global Multi-Brand Reputation Audit

## Context

Paysafe, a multinational online payments company (payment processing, digital wallets, online cash solutions for global entertainment sectors), wanted to understand their online reputation across multiple brands and territories. The goal was to uncover detailed insights and gain actionable recommendations to improve reputation and competitive edge.

## Issue Type

- No clear picture of online reputation across brands and regions
- Needed competitive positioning analysis
- Multiple sub-brands requiring separate assessment
- 13 key executives' personal reputations to evaluate

## Actions Taken

1. **In-Depth Global Audit**: 3-month audit using proprietary Reputation Index and specialist tools covering:
   - **Paysafecard** (incl. Paysafecash) in: Germany, Greece, Switzerland, France, Italy, Austria, Spain, Netherlands, UK, Belgium
   - **Neteller** in: Bangladesh, India, UK, Korea (South), Germany, Greece, Vietnam, Portugal, Spain, Tunisia
   - **Skrill** in: Italy, UK, Germany, Russia, Spain, Malta, Netherlands, India, Austria, Ukraine
   - **13 Key Executives**: Individual reputation assessments
2. **Competitor Comparison**: Benchmarked Paysafe and sub-brands against close competitors
3. **Recommendations Report**: Delivered detailed analysis with comprehensive action plan

## Outcome

- Paysafe and sub-brands scored well against competitors overall
- Identified many areas of opportunity to expand share of voice and improve brand reputation
- Delivered actionable recommendations for review channel and forum management
- Provided clear roadmap for reputation improvement across territories

## Lessons Learned

- Multi-brand, multi-territory audits require systematic coverage — each brand+country combination has different search landscapes
- Executive reputation directly impacts company reputation, especially in financial services — auditing both is essential
- A proprietary reputation index allows benchmarking against competitors with consistent methodology
- Even companies that score well have opportunities — reputation management is about share of voice, not just absence of negatives
- A 3-month timeline is realistic for comprehensive global audits covering 20+ countries
- The audit deliverable itself (board-ready report) is as important as the findings — it must be actionable
