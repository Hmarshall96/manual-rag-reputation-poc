# Global Nuclear Fuel Company - Stakeholder Reputation Review

## Context

A global nuclear fuel company selected Igniyte to conduct its first-ever full Reputation Review across 14 stakeholder groups in 4 territories. The company wanted to understand its reputation, gain stakeholder opinions about the brand, communications, and CSR activities, and obtain benchmarks to inform future global and local strategies.

## Issue Type

- No existing measurement of stakeholder perception
- Need for multi-territory, multi-stakeholder reputation baseline
- Desire to benchmark communication quality and CSR effectiveness
- Required board-ready deliverable to inform strategy

## Actions Taken

1. **Initial Research**: Studied the business, its audiences, communications, and stakeholder engagement
2. **Online Reputation Index**: Assessed online reputation of the whole business by region and for each executive team member
3. **Bespoke Surveys**: Created and delivered 36 bespoke online surveys reflecting 14 stakeholder groups — investors, politicians, environmental agencies, suppliers, customers, schools, and local communities
4. **Multilingual Execution**: Each survey conducted in the stakeholder's native language — English, Dutch, or German
5. **Analysis & Reporting**: Collated quantitative and qualitative data, delivered final report with detailed insights and recommendations, and presented board-ready presentation

## Outcome

- 40% stakeholder survey response rate (far exceeding the 10-15% target)
- Global and country-level reputation scores established
- Stakeholder Net Promoter Score (NPS) calculated
- Brand perception benchmarks created
- Communication quality ratings delivered (quantitative + qualitative)
- Igniyte commissioned to conduct the follow-up Reputation Review in 2019

## Lessons Learned

- First-ever reputation reviews benefit from setting a low response rate target — the actual engagement (40% vs 10-15% target) often exceeds expectations when surveys are well-designed
- Multilingual survey execution is essential for authentic stakeholder feedback in non-English territories
- Stakeholder groups vary enormously (investors vs. local communities vs. environmental agencies) — bespoke surveys per group yield more actionable insights than one-size-fits-all approaches
- Online Reputation Index assessment at both company and executive level reveals reputation gaps that might not surface in aggregate
- A board-ready presentation is a critical deliverable format — it ensures findings reach decision-makers directly
- Reputation reviews should be recurring (the follow-up was commissioned 2 years later) to track changes against benchmarks
