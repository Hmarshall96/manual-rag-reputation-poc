# UK C-Suite Executive - Employment Tribunal Press Coverage

## Context

A former C-suite executive at a FTSE100 UK supermarket chain contacted Igniyte after a high-profile employment tribunal generated extensive negative press coverage. When searching her name in Google, Pages 1-2 were dominated by links to negative news articles, press images of her attending court, and defamatory forum comments. Google Images was also dominated by negative court photos.

In her words: "The issue was one of misinformation and reporting on a legal matter I was involved in."

## Issue Type

- High-profile employment tribunal coverage in UK national press
- Negative court attendance images in search results and image libraries
- Defamatory forum comments
- Google Images dominated by negative photos
- 13 out of 20 links on Google Page 1-2 were negative at project start

## Actions Taken

### Content Removal (16 articles + 20 images)
Successfully de-indexed or deleted-at-source 16 negative news articles from:
- The Times, Belfast Telegraph, The Grocer, Retail Gazette, HR Magazine, and other trade publications

Successfully de-indexed or deleted-at-source 20 negative images from:
- Alamy, Shutterstock, Evening Chronicle, Manchester Evening News, Daily Mail, Financial Times

### Personal Brand Rebuild
- Developed a professional website for her Non-Executive Director consultancy
- Created and optimized multiple professional social media profiles (LinkedIn)

### PR and Thought Leadership
- Secured 8 media interviews in quality publications: Raconteur, Procurement Magazine, People Management, Compliance Week
- Secured multiple podcast interviews on her area of specialism to distance her from unwanted past

## Outcome

- Google Page 1-3 completely cleared of all negative articles and images within 4 months
- 20 of 20 links on Page 1-2 became positive client-owned content
- Negative results reduced from 90% to 0%
- New professional identity established as Non-Executive Director consultant
- Complete repositioning from tribunal-associated individual to industry thought leader

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Before | After |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Negative results (Page 1-2) | 90% (13/20 links) | 0% |
| Articles removed | - | 16 |
| Images removed | - | 20 |
| New media interviews | - | 8 |
| Timeline | - | 4 months |

## Lessons Learned

- Image removal is as critical as article removal — Google Images can be equally damaging and is often overlooked
- Press photo libraries (Alamy, Shutterstock) index court images that persist long after the event — these must be addressed separately from article removals
- Repositioning requires building a new narrative, not just removing the old one — the podcast and thought leadership strategy created an alternative identity
- Weekly project meetings with proactive "drumbeat" execution kept momentum and compressed the 4-month timeline
- For individuals, a professional website + LinkedIn profile serve as the foundation for controlling Page 1 results
- Budget flexibility matters — the client noted Igniyte "takes into consideration budget constraints" with a "very flexible process"
