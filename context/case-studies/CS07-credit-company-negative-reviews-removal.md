# UK Credit Management Company - Negative Reviews and Forum Removal

## Context

A leading UK credit management (debt collection) company faced unfair comments online, primarily stemming from the negative sentiment inherently associated with their industry. They also wanted to rank higher for positive and lucrative search terms. At project start, 11 out of 20 links on Google Page 1-2 were negative reviews and forum comments, causing the company to lose business opportunities.

## Issue Type

- Negative reviews and forum comments (11/20 results on Page 1-2)
- Industry-inherent negative sentiment (debt collection)
- Low search visibility for positive, lucrative search terms
- Lost business opportunities due to negative search presence

## Actions Taken

1. **Full Online Reputation Audit**: Assessed the complete search landscape and identified offending links
2. **Google Removal Requests**: Submitted successful removal requests to take down offending links
3. **Website Optimization**: Created, optimized, and promoted a new company website
4. **Professional Profiles**: Built and optimized professional online profiles across relevant platforms
5. **Industry E-Guides**: Created and promoted educational content guides for the industry
6. **National Media Coverage**: Secured high-ranking coverage in The Times, Reuters, and Yahoo Finance through targeted media strategy

## Outcome

- Negative results reduced from 45% to 20% of Page 1-2
- Successful Google removal requests processed
- Growing online share of voice for lucrative search terms
- High-ranking national media coverage established

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Before | After |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Negative results (Page 1-2) | 45% (11/20) | 20% |
| Media coverage | None | The Times, Reuters, Yahoo Finance |
| Professional profiles | Limited | Optimized and promoted |
| Industry e-guides | None | Created and ranking |

## Lessons Learned

- Industries with inherently negative sentiment (debt collection, legal, enforcement) require ongoing reputation management — negative reviews are structural, not situational
- Not all negative results can be removed — reducing from 45% to 20% was the realistic outcome, with the remaining negatives suppressed rather than eliminated
- Industry e-guides serve dual purpose: they demonstrate thought leadership AND rank for valuable search terms, displacing negative content
- National media coverage (The Times, Reuters) provides high-authority backlinks that outrank forum comments and review sites
- For companies in stigmatized industries, the strategy must combine removal (where possible) with volume suppression through quality content
