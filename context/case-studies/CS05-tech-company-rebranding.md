# UK Technology Company - Rebranding for Trust and Credibility

## Context

A UK technology company needed an updated brand identity and website to increase trust, credibility, and sales online. Their existing brand was outdated and failed to communicate their newly defined vision. The brand needed to be bold, disruptive, and authentic while inspiring a broad demographic.

## Issue Type

- Weak and outdated brand identity
- Low trust and credibility online
- Website not generating sufficient leads or conversions
- Brand story not reflected in online presence

## Actions Taken

1. **Brand Identity**: Created new logo, brand collateral, and visual identity aligned with newly defined vision
2. **Website Redesign**: Full website design, UX optimization, and technical build including:
   - Flexible templates for content management
   - Multiple landing pages for inbound leads
   - Increased landing page conversion rates
   - Full HubSpot CRM integration
3. **Brand Messaging**: Developed communications that reflected the brand story and business personality

## Outcome

- Increase in turnover by £7 million
- Changed internal and external perception of the business
- Opened new business opportunities
- Increased order value, revenue, and profit
- Industry-leading, disruptive brand presence

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Turnover increase | £7 million |
| Brand perception | Transformed internally and externally |
| Lead generation | Multiple landing pages with improved conversion |

## Lessons Learned

- Brand reputation starts with brand identity — if the visual identity and website don't convey credibility, no amount of SEO will compensate
- Website UX and CRM integration (HubSpot) directly impact conversion rates and revenue
- Rebranding is a valid reputation strategy when the issue is not negative content but weak/invisible presence
- Internal perception matters as much as external — employees who believe in the brand become advocates
- For B2B technology companies, a professional website is often the first impression during procurement evaluation
