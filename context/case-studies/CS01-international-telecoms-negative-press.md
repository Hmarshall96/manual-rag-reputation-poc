# CCI Global - International Telecoms Negative Press Campaign

## Context

CCI Global is the largest international call centre operator in Africa (~$180M revenue, clients include Vodafone, AT&T, Netflix, 17 call centres across Africa). They faced a significant negative reputation crisis when negative news articles, social media posts, and online campaign pages (Change.org) dominated Google Page 1-2 results for the search term "CCI Kenya" in both UK and US Google Search.

Two of their Kenyan call centres were the subject of negative articles about workforce practices (false/defamatory). This was deterring potential clients during tendering processes and significantly harming revenue.

## Issue Type

- Negative news articles dominating branded search results
- Activist campaign pages (Change.org petition)
- Social media posts amplifying negative narrative
- False/defamatory workforce practice allegations

## Starting Position

At project start (May 2023), Google Page 1-2 for "CCI Kenya" showed approximately 90% negative or damaging results, including:
- Negative news articles on English-speaking African news sites
- A Change.org petition about working conditions
- Negative Glassdoor reviews
- TikTok content with negative reviews

## Actions Taken

1. **Google Removals**: Forced deletion of 3 negative articles from Change.org and African news websites via legal teams
2. **PR Content Strategy**: Placed 10+ high-quality SEO-optimized articles in publications including Internet Retailing, Digital Commerce, Finance Monthly, and Chain Store Age
3. **Professional Profile Development**: Created 10+ industry profiles for the brand across high-authority platforms (Crunchbase, Medium, LinkedIn, etc.)
4. **Social Media Campaigns**: Integrated campaigns to help dominate search rankings for the target keyword
5. **Ongoing PR**: After initial suppression, generated 50+ credible global news features in Business Insider, Associated Press, Global Finance, CMS Wire, Telecompaper, and African Business

## Outcome

- Google Page 1-2 completely cleared of all negative results
- 20 out of 20 top search results dominated by positive/neutral content
- Transformed from ~90% negative to 0% negative on Pages 1-2
- Client was so satisfied they contracted Igniyte as full-time PR and online reputation agency
- 50+ positive global stories secured in subsequent months

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Before | After |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Negative results (Page 1-2) | ~90% (multiple negative) | 0% |
| Positive/neutral results | ~10% | 100% |
| Articles removed | - | 3 |
| PR placements | - | 10+ initial, 50+ ongoing |
| Industry profiles created | - | 10+ |

## Lessons Learned

- A combined removal + suppression strategy is most effective when negative content is both defamatory and widely distributed
- High-authority PR placements in industry-relevant publications outrank negative articles over time
- Professional profile creation across multiple platforms helps fill search results with controlled content
- Speed matters: working "at pace" to build content while simultaneously pursuing removals compressed the timeline
- Success in ORM can lead to ongoing PR retainer relationships — demonstrating ROI opens the door to continued engagement
- For international companies, PR coverage must target the same geographic Google results (US/UK) where potential clients search
