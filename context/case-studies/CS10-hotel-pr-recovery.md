# Niche European Hotel - PR and Visibility Recovery

## Context

A luxury hotel in Provence, France approached Igniyte to increase its online visibility through positive PR and social engagement. The hotel wanted to dominate Google search results and promote its products and services. It had limited online presence, low domain authority, and weak social media engagement.

## Issue Type

- Limited online visibility for a niche hospitality business
- Low domain authority on hotel website
- Weak social media presence and engagement
- Difficulty reaching UK travel journalists and media

## Actions Taken

1. **Traffic & Authority Building**: Increased traffic to the hotel's main websites and site links to improve influence and domain authority
2. **Paid Social Campaigns**: Ran paid social media campaigns sharing offers and blog content to increase engagement
3. **Profile Optimization**: Audited and optimized existing digital profiles and blog
4. **Content Creation**: Created and distributed new, regular content across channels
5. **Press Trip**: Organized a full press trip for UK travel journalists to the property
6. **Media Coverage**: Secured high-ranking press coverage in 3 UK travel journals (including Silverspoon London)

## Outcome

- Increased traffic, site links, website influence, and domain authority over 6 months
- 118 new outbound links gained
- Positive high-ranking coverage in 3 UK travel journals
- Increased social media engagement and followers
- Improved customer communications
- Hotel's profile raised online — dominating Google search results

## Lessons Learned

- For hospitality businesses, press trips are one of the highest-ROI reputation activities — journalists create high-authority, richly linked content
- Combining organic content with paid social amplification accelerates visibility gains
- Domain authority building requires consistent effort over months (6-month engagement) — there are no shortcuts
- Regular content creation and distribution establishes a pattern that search engines reward with higher rankings
- For niche businesses in competitive tourism markets, UK travel journalists provide access to a high-value, high-search-volume audience
- Profile optimization across existing digital properties is low-hanging fruit that should be done before creating new content
