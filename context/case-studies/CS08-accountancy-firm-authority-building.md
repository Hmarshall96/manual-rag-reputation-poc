# UK Accountancy Firm - Building Authority and Trust Online

## Context

A large UK accountancy firm wanted to increase its authority and credibility online. The firm had low online visibility, underutilized social media channels, and limited thought leadership presence. Three negative results appeared on Page 1, and the firm's partners were not positioned as industry experts.

## Issue Type

- Low authority and credibility online
- Underutilized social media presence
- Limited thought leadership content
- Negative results on Page 1 (3 items)
- Partners not recognized as industry experts

## Actions Taken

1. **SEO**: Optimized the company website and all social media profiles for both the business and a key executive
2. **Social Media Strategy**: Built presence across LinkedIn, Twitter, YouTube, Facebook, and Google+ with high-quality, engaging content
3. **Thought Leadership Content**: Created content positioning directors as accountancy and financial services thought leaders
4. **Employee Advocacy**: Encouraged the company and employees to increase sharing of news and updates
5. **Content Creation**: Produced fresh, high-quality content targeting key search terms
6. **PR Placement**: Published guest blogs on topics including business and human rights, gaining coverage in regional and trade press

## Outcome

- Firm and key director established as industry thought leaders
- High-ranking and high-engagement social media channels
- Positive PR, online profiles, and e-guides on accountancy news, business, and human rights published
- 3 negative results suppressed — positive and owned Page 1 at project end
- Quoted in regional and trade press

## Lessons Learned

- Proactive reputation building is easier and cheaper than reactive crisis management — building authority before a crisis prevents one
- Employee advocacy multiplies content reach without additional content creation cost
- Multi-platform social media presence (LinkedIn, YouTube, Facebook, Twitter) creates multiple Page 1 results that the company controls
- Thought leadership content on adjacent topics (e.g., human rights for an accountancy firm) differentiates and attracts higher-authority publication opportunities
- For professional services firms, individual partner/director reputation and firm reputation are inseparable — building both simultaneously is essential
