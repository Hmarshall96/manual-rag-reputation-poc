# A06 - Reputation Audit & Monitoring

## Overview

A reputation audit is the diagnostic foundation of every reputation management engagement. Before any strategy can be developed, you must understand the current state of the client's online reputation in granular detail. What appears when someone searches their name? What is the sentiment of those results? Which platforms carry the most influence? Where are the vulnerabilities?

Monitoring is the ongoing discipline that follows the initial audit. Reputation is not static — new content appears, search rankings shift, reviews accumulate, and social media conversations evolve. Without continuous monitoring, a reputation management campaign operates blind.

This guide covers both the initial audit process and the ongoing monitoring framework, linking to the specific how-to guides for execution.

## Applicable Scenarios

- **S11 - New client requiring initial assessment**: The starting point for every engagement
- **S13 - Ongoing reputation maintenance**: Continuous monitoring for established clients

This guide also supports every other scenario indirectly, because an audit is the prerequisite for determining which action guides (A01-A10) to deploy.

## When to Conduct a Reputation Audit

### New Client Onboarding

Every new client engagement begins with a comprehensive reputation audit. This is non-negotiable. Without understanding the baseline, you cannot set meaningful goals, recommend the right strategies, or measure progress. See **A10 - Client Onboarding & Goal Setting** for how the audit fits into the onboarding process.

### Post-Crisis Assessment

After a crisis (see **A03 - Crisis Communications Response**), conduct a fresh audit to understand the full impact. Crisis-related content may appear across search results, reviews, social media, and news coverage. The post-crisis audit maps this damage and informs the recovery strategy.

### Quarterly or Monthly Review

For ongoing clients, conduct a lighter-weight audit at regular intervals — monthly for active campaigns, quarterly for maintenance-phase clients. This catches shifts in the reputation landscape and identifies new threats or opportunities.

### Competitive Analysis

Auditing competitors' reputations provides context. If a client's Google Reviews average is 3.8, that looks different depending on whether competitors average 3.2 or 4.5. Competitive benchmarking helps set realistic targets and identify industry-specific reputation dynamics.

## Components of a Full Reputation Audit

### 1. SERP Audit

The search engine results page audit is the most critical component. Map the top 20 Google results (Pages 1 and 2) for every relevant search term.

**Search terms to audit:**
- Full name (individual) or company name
- Name + location (e.g., "John Smith Manchester")
- Name + industry or role (e.g., "John Smith solicitor")
- Company name + "reviews"
- Common misspellings of the name
- Name + any known negative keywords (e.g., "Company Name scandal")

**For each result, record:**
- Position (1-20)
- URL
- Page title and meta description
- Sentiment: positive, neutral, or negative
- Domain authority of the hosting site
- Date of publication (if applicable)
- Whether the client controls this property
- Whether the result is removable (see **A01**)

See **H27 - Conducting a Reputation Audit** for the detailed methodology and templates.

### 2. Social Media Audit

Review the client's presence across all major social media platforms:

- Does the profile exist? Is it claimed by the client or by someone else?
- Is the profile complete and optimized?
- What is the posting frequency and engagement level?
- What is the sentiment of comments and mentions?
- Are there any problematic posts from the client's own history?
- What are others saying about the client on social media?

Key platforms to audit: LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, Reddit, Crunchbase, and any industry-specific platforms.

### 3. Review Audit

Audit all relevant review platforms:

- **Average star rating** on each platform
- **Total number of reviews** on each platform
- **Sentiment breakdown**: What percentage are positive (4-5 stars), neutral (3 stars), and negative (1-2 stars)?
- **Recent trend**: Are recent reviews better or worse than historical ones?
- **Response rate**: What percentage of reviews have responses from the business?
- **Flaggable reviews**: Are any reviews clearly fake, defamatory, or policy-violating?

Platforms to audit: Google Reviews, Trustpilot, TripAdvisor (if applicable), Glassdoor, industry-specific review sites.

See **A04 - Review Management & Response** for how to act on findings.

### 4. Sentiment Analysis

Beyond individual results, assess the overall sentiment narrative. What story does Google tell about this person or company? Is the dominant narrative positive, neutral, mixed, or negative? Are there specific themes that recur across multiple results (e.g., "poor customer service" appearing in reviews, articles, and forum posts)?

Sentiment analysis should consider:
- The overall tone of Page 1 results
- Whether negative results are growing or shrinking in prominence
- The sources of negative sentiment (are they authoritative or fringe?)
- Whether the client has any positive narrative to build on

### 5. Competitor Benchmarking

For business clients, audit 3-5 key competitors using the same methodology. This provides:

- Context for the client's current position (are they better or worse than the industry average?)
- Identification of platforms and strategies that competitors use effectively
- Realistic benchmarks for goal setting

## Setting Up Ongoing Monitoring

Once the initial audit is complete, establish a monitoring framework to track changes continuously. See **H28 - Setting Up Reputation Monitoring** for the technical setup.

### Monitoring Tools and Methods

**Google Alerts**: Set up alerts for the client's name, company name, key executives, and any relevant keywords. Google Alerts are free but not comprehensive — they catch news and blog mentions but miss social media and forums.

**Social listening tools**: Tools like Mention, Brandwatch, or Hootsuite Insights track social media mentions in real time. These are essential for catching emerging issues before they escalate.

**Review monitoring**: Set up notifications on all review platforms so new reviews are flagged immediately. Many review platforms offer email alerts for new reviews.

**SERP tracking**: Use rank tracking tools (e.g., SEMrush, Ahrefs, or BrightLocal) to monitor search result positions for target keywords on a weekly basis. This is the primary metric for measuring campaign success.

**News monitoring**: For corporate clients, media monitoring services (e.g., Meltwater, Cision) provide comprehensive coverage of news mentions across print, broadcast, and online media.

### Monitoring Cadence

- **Daily**: Check Google Alerts and social media mentions. Respond to new reviews.
- **Weekly**: Review SERP positions for all target keywords. Note any significant movements.
- **Monthly**: Produce a monitoring report summarising key changes, metrics, and recommended actions.
- **Quarterly**: Conduct a mini-audit to refresh the baseline and assess overall progress.

### Alert Escalation Framework

Not all monitoring alerts require the same response. Establish a tiered system:

- **Tier 1 - Urgent (respond within 4 hours)**: New negative article on a high-authority site, viral social media post, crisis-level event, new defamatory content
- **Tier 2 - Important (respond within 24 hours)**: New negative review, negative social media mention with moderate engagement, minor SERP position changes
- **Tier 3 - Routine (respond within 1 week)**: Positive mentions, stable SERP positions, routine review responses, general sentiment tracking

## Deliverables

### Initial Audit Report

The initial audit should produce a comprehensive report including:

- Executive summary of findings
- SERP maps for all target search terms
- Review scorecard across all platforms
- Social media presence assessment
- Sentiment analysis summary
- Competitor benchmarking (if applicable)
- Risk assessment: what could get worse?
- Recommended strategy with prioritised actions
- Estimated timeline and resource requirements

### Ongoing Monitoring Dashboard

For active clients, provide a dashboard or regular report that tracks:

- SERP positions for all target keywords (with trend arrows)
- Review metrics across all platforms (rating, volume, sentiment)
- Social media mention volume and sentiment
- Media coverage log
- Actions taken and their outcomes
- Upcoming actions and priorities

### Regular Reporting

Establish a reporting cadence with the client:

- **Weekly**: Brief email update on key movements and any required actions
- **Monthly**: Full monitoring report with metrics, analysis, and recommendations
- **Quarterly**: Strategic review meeting to assess progress against goals and adjust the plan

## Combining With Other Strategies

The audit and monitoring function supports every other action guide:

- **Content removal (A01)**: The audit identifies removable content
- **SEO suppression (A02)**: SERP tracking measures suppression effectiveness
- **Crisis communications (A03)**: Monitoring provides early warning of emerging crises
- **Review management (A04)**: Review auditing identifies platforms and issues to address
- **Social media (A05)**: The social media audit identifies gaps and opportunities
- **Legal escalation (A07)**: The audit identifies content that may warrant legal action
- **Client onboarding (A10)**: The audit is the centrepiece of the onboarding process
