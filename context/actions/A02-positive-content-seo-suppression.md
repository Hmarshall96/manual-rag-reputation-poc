# A02 - Positive Content Creation & SEO Suppression

## Overview

Positive content creation and SEO suppression is the cornerstone of reputation management when content cannot be removed. The strategy is straightforward in principle: create enough high-quality, well-optimized positive content that it outranks the negative results on Google's first two pages. In practice, this requires disciplined execution across multiple content types, platforms, and SEO techniques over a sustained period.

This is not a quick fix. SEO suppression campaigns typically take 3-6 months to show meaningful movement and 6-12 months for comprehensive results. However, it is the most reliable long-term strategy because it builds durable assets that the client controls, rather than depending on third parties to remove content.

## Applicable Scenarios

This action guide applies to the following scenarios:

- **S01 - Defamatory content about an individual**: When removal is not possible or is being pursued in parallel
- **S06 - Negative business reviews dominating search**: When review scores cannot be changed quickly enough
- **S08 - Executive or founder reputation**: Building a strong personal brand to push down negatives
- **S09 - Corporate crisis aftermath**: Rebuilding a company's search presence after a crisis
- **S12 - New business or individual seeking proactive reputation**: Building a positive presence from scratch

## The Core Principle

Google displays approximately 10 organic results per page. The goal is to control as many of those 20 slots (Pages 1 and 2) as possible with content that is positive, neutral, or at minimum not harmful to the client's reputation.

Most users never look past Page 1. Research consistently shows that fewer than 5% of searchers click through to Page 2. This means that pushing a negative result from position 5 to position 11 (the top of Page 2) dramatically reduces its visibility, even though the content still exists.

Each piece of content you create and optimize is competing for one of those 20 slots. The more slots you fill with content you control, the fewer slots are available for negative content.

## Content Types and Platforms

Different content types have different ranking potential. Prioritize in this order:

### Tier 1 - Highest Ranking Potential

- **Personal or company website**: The single most important asset. A well-optimized website targeting the client's name will almost always rank in the top 3. See **H20 - Website SEO Optimization** and **H23 - WordPress Site Setup for Reputation Management**.
- **LinkedIn profile**: LinkedIn has enormous domain authority and consistently ranks on Page 1 for name searches. See **H24 - LinkedIn Profile Optimization**.
- **Crunchbase profile** (for business leaders): High domain authority, frequently indexed.
- **Google Business Profile** (for companies): Triggers the knowledge panel and map pack.

### Tier 2 - Strong Ranking Potential

- **PR articles on high-authority news sites**: Placements in Forbes, The Guardian, industry publications, and regional news outlets. See **H18 - PR Article Placement** and **H22 - Thought Leadership Content Creation**.
- **Medium articles**: Medium's domain authority helps articles rank quickly for long-tail terms.
- **YouTube videos**: Google owns YouTube and frequently surfaces video results. Interview-style or explainer videos work well.
- **Twitter/X profile**: Profiles often rank for name searches. See **H25 - Social Media Profile Setup and Optimization**.

### Tier 3 - Supporting Content

- **Guest posts on industry blogs**: Build backlinks and create additional ranking pages.
- **Podcast appearances**: Show notes pages often rank, and podcast platforms have high authority.
- **Industry directory listings**: Relevant for professionals (e.g., legal directories, medical directories).
- **Image results**: Optimized professional photography can dominate the image carousel.
- **SlideShare presentations**: Useful for thought leadership positioning.

## Strategic Decision Factors

Before creating content, answer these questions:

### What search terms need suppression?

Audit the current SERP landscape for every relevant search term. The client's full name, company name, name + location, name + industry, and common misspellings all need assessing. Different search terms may require different strategies. See **A06 - Reputation Audit & Monitoring** for the audit process.

### What content types already rank well?

If LinkedIn profiles tend to rank highly for the client's name, optimizing LinkedIn is a priority. If news articles dominate the results, PR placements are the best path to displacement. Work with the grain of what Google already favours for that query.

### What high-authority platforms accept content?

Not all platforms are equally accessible. Some require editorial approval, others are pay-to-play, and some are open to anyone. Map out which platforms are realistic targets for the client's industry and profile.

### What is the current domain authority of competing negative content?

A negative article on a high-authority news site (DA 80+) is much harder to suppress than a blog post on a low-authority personal site (DA 20). The higher the authority of the negative content, the more high-authority positive content you need to outrank it.

## Execution Framework

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

1. **Audit the current SERP landscape** for all target search terms (see **A06**)
2. **Set up or optimize the client's website** with proper on-page SEO, schema markup, and content targeting the client's name (see **H20**, **H23**)
3. **Claim and optimize all social media profiles** across major platforms (see **H24**, **H25**)
4. **Create a content calendar** mapping out what will be published, where, and when

### Phase 2: Content Production (Weeks 4-16)

5. **Publish 2-4 PR articles** on high-authority sites targeting key search terms (see **H18**)
6. **Create thought leadership content** — guest articles, blog posts, opinion pieces (see **H22**)
7. **Produce multimedia content** — videos, podcasts, presentations
8. **Build backlinks** to all positive content to increase its authority (see **H20**)

### Phase 3: Amplification (Weeks 8-24)

9. **Continue publishing** at a steady cadence — consistency matters more than volume
10. **Build additional backlinks** to content that is close to Page 1 but not yet ranking
11. **Monitor SERP positions** weekly and adjust strategy based on what is moving (see **H28**)
12. **Refresh and update** existing content to keep it current and signal freshness to Google

### Phase 4: Maintenance (Ongoing)

13. **Publish new content monthly** to maintain momentum
14. **Monitor for new negative content** that could displace positive results
15. **Update profiles and website** regularly to prevent stagnation

## Key Metrics to Track

- **SERP positions** for all target search terms (tracked weekly)
- **Number of Page 1 results controlled** by the client (the primary success metric)
- **Domain authority** of the client's website and key content pages
- **Backlink profile** growth over time
- **Organic traffic** to the client's website
- **Position of the highest-ranking negative result** (the goal is to push this to Page 2 or beyond)

## Volume Requirements

For meaningful suppression, expect to create:

- **Minimum viable campaign**: 10-15 pieces of content across 8-10 platforms
- **Standard campaign**: 20-30 pieces of content across 12-15 platforms
- **Comprehensive transformation**: 50+ pieces of content across 20+ platforms (see the CCI Global case study for an example of this scale)

## When This Strategy Is Most Effective

SEO suppression works best when:

- Negative content is on lower-authority sites (DA below 50)
- The client has existing assets (website, social profiles) that can be optimized
- There is genuine positive content to create — real achievements, expertise, and stories to tell
- The client is willing to commit to a 6-12 month timeline
- Multiple search terms need improvement (more opportunities for quick wins)

## When to Combine With Other Strategies

SEO suppression should almost always be combined with:

- **Content removal (A01)**: Remove what you can, suppress what you cannot
- **Social media optimization (A05)**: Profiles are both content assets and engagement channels
- **Technical SEO (A09)**: The foundation that makes all content perform better
- **Thought leadership (A08)**: PR placements serve both suppression and brand-building goals
