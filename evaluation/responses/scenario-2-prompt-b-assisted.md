## 1. Immediate action plan for the next 24 hours

### First 60 minutes
**1. Activate a crisis team now.**  
Use the [H15] model and appoint, by name:

- Crisis Lead: CEO
- Spokesperson: ideally one trained senior executive, not multiple people [H15][H16]
- Legal adviser: external regulatory/litigation counsel
- Operations lead: head of ops / food safety lead
- Communications lead: PR/comms lead or agency
- Digital lead: social/community manager [H15]

**2. Freeze the message environment.**  
- Pause all scheduled content across every channel [H17]
- Instruct all staff: **no posting, no commenting, no speaking to media**
- Route all press calls to one inbox/mobile number [H15][H17]

**3. Secure the site and the evidence.**  
- Keep the Manchester site closed unless and until legal/regulatory advisers say otherwise
- Preserve CCTV, kitchen logs, temperature checks, supplier delivery notes, rota data, cleaning schedules, training records, allergen records, complaint logs
- Start a contemporaneous incident log: every decision, timestamp, and approver

**4. Issue a holding statement immediately.**  
This is the top priority if it has not already gone out. [H16] is explicit that silence gets read as evasion.

### Hours 1–4
**5. Get verified facts, not theories.**  
Build a live factsheet covering:

- number of affected customers confirmed by authorities/hospitals
- what dates/times they dined
- whether one meal period/product is implicated
- status of Manchester site
- whether any other site has linked complaints
- what regulators have requested
- what support is being offered to affected customers

**6. Notify and cooperate with authorities.**  
Your external position should be cooperative and non-defensive. Food businesses are expected to work with local authorities during suspected food poisoning incidents, and outbreak management in England is coordinated across local health protection, environmental health, FSA, and UKHSA structures. ([food.gov.uk](https://www.food.gov.uk/food-safety-and-hygiene/food-poisoning?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

**7. Commission an urgent food-safety review across all 12 sites.**  
Not because you know the cause, but because you need visible precautionary action. Check:

- temperature logs
- supplier traceability
- cleaning and sanitising records
- staff illness reporting
- handwashing compliance
- segregation / cross-contamination controls
- hot-hold / chilled-hold compliance
- high-risk ingredients common to all branches

The FSA expects businesses to have food safety management procedures based on HACCP principles. ([food.gov.uk](https://www.food.gov.uk/business-guidance/business-guidance/food-safety-management-for-businesses?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

### Hours 4–12
**8. Send an all-staff instruction memo and hold manager calls.**  
Staff anxiety is already becoming a secondary crisis. [S07] and [H15] both warn that uninformed employees create leaks, contradictions, and speculation [S07][H15].

Message to staff should include:

- only the spokesperson may comment externally
- do not speculate online
- do not discuss customers’ health or identities
- refer media/public queries centrally
- branch managers will receive approved customer-facing language

**9. Publish one pinned update on every social platform.**  
Use the same core wording as the holding statement, adapted slightly for platform length [H17].

**10. Decline live interviews for now; offer a written statement.**  
For the two national journalists, the CEO should not do a live or probing interview yet unless fully briefed and media-trained. Offer:
- written statement now
- next update time
- later recorded interview only if facts improve

### Hours 12–24
**11. Launch stakeholder communications in parallel.**
Send tailored messages to:

- affected customers / families
- current bookings
- staff
- suppliers
- landlords
- insurers
- lenders / investors if relevant

**12. Announce concrete steps, not generic concern.**  
By the end of day one, you want to be able to say:

- Manchester site voluntarily closed pending investigation
- independent food-safety specialists appointed
- group-wide precautionary audits underway
- support line/email established
- cooperation with environmental health ongoing

**13. Establish an update cadence.**  
Follow a CERC-style rhythm: early messages should express empathy, explain what you know, what you do not yet know, and what actions people should take. ([cdc.gov](https://www.cdc.gov/cerc/php/cerc-manual/index.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

## 2. Ready-to-use holding statement for media

Below is a version that follows the [H16] structure: acknowledge, express concern, state action, promise updates [H16].

> **Holding statement for immediate release**
>
> “We are deeply concerned by reports that a number of customers became unwell after visiting our Manchester restaurant, and our thoughts are with everyone affected.
>
> We can confirm that we are cooperating fully with the local authority’s environmental health team and supporting their investigation as a priority.
>
> As a precaution, we have closed our Manchester restaurant while we work urgently to understand what happened. We have also begun immediate checks across our wider estate.
>
> The safety and wellbeing of our customers and staff is our highest priority. Because this is an active investigation, it would be inappropriate to speculate on the cause at this stage.
>
> We will provide further updates as soon as we have verified information to share.”
>
> — **[CEO name], Chief Executive**

### Optional second paragraph for customer channels
> “Any customer who is concerned and believes they may have been affected should contact [dedicated phone/email/web page] so we can support them directly.”

### What the CEO should say if pressed live
Use only three lines:
1. “Our priority is the wellbeing of those affected.”  
2. “We are cooperating fully with environmental health.”  
3. “We will not speculate until the facts are established.”

## 3. Social media response strategy

[H17] is the right operating manual here: pause scheduled content, monitor continuously, use the holding statement only, and **do not delete legitimate criticism** [H17].

### Channel strategy for the next 7 days
**Pinned post on X, Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn**
- same core statement everywhere
- comments left on where manageable
- link to one central updates page

**Response rules**
- respond only where there is a question, direct accusation, or welfare concern
- do not argue
- do not copy-paste robotically on every thread
- remove only threats, hate speech, doxxing, or personal medical information [H17]

**Monitoring buckets**
1. welfare concerns from customers
2. media/journalist enquiries
3. misinformation/false claims
4. employee posts
5. coordinated trolling / pile-ons
6. legal-risk comments naming individuals or alleging facts not in evidence

### Tone
Use a mix of **empathy + action + boundaries**.  
This is also consistent with CERC: express empathy, be first, be credible, promote action, show respect. ([cdc.gov](https://www.cdc.gov/cerc/php/cerc-manual/index.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

### Template responses

#### A. Angry customer
> We’re very sorry to read this. We’re treating this with the utmost seriousness and are cooperating fully with the environmental health investigation. Please send your contact details to [email/DM route] so our team can support you directly.

#### B. “Your food made people seriously ill”
> We understand why people are deeply concerned. Our Manchester restaurant is closed as a precaution while the investigation is underway, and we’re cooperating fully with the relevant authorities. We’ll share further verified updates as soon as we can.

#### C. “This happened because your company doesn’t care about hygiene”
> We understand the concern. It would be inappropriate to speculate while the investigation is active, but we are taking immediate action, including precautionary checks and full cooperation with environmental health.

#### D. “Is it safe to eat at your other branches?”
> We understand why customers are asking this. We have initiated immediate checks across our wider estate as a precaution. At present, the investigation relates to our Manchester restaurant, and we will provide updates if that position changes.

#### E. Request for refund / compensation
> Please contact [dedicated email/phone/form] with your visit details so our customer support team can review this urgently.

#### F. Journalist on social media
> Thanks for your message. Please send media enquiries to [press email/mobile]. Our current statement is here: [link].

#### G. Misinformation / false viral claim
> We want to correct one point: [brief factual correction]. We are cooperating fully with the ongoing investigation and will continue to share verified updates through our official channels.

#### H. Abusive / profane comment
No public back-and-forth. Hide or moderate only if it breaches platform rules or includes threats/doxxing [H17].

#### I. Staff posting publicly
Ask them to delete only if they reveal confidential info or breach policy; otherwise handle internally and immediately issue the staff note. Do not start a public argument with employees.

## 6. How to handle the Trustpilot review bombing

Treat this as both a **compliance issue** and a **reputation issue**.

### What to do immediately
**1. Claim and actively manage the business profile.**  
Respond publicly, calmly, and consistently.

**2. Audit incoming reviews by category.**
Split into:
- genuine customer complaints
- duplicate reviews
- clearly fake/non-customer reviews
- media-driven pile-on with no genuine experience
- abusive / defamatory / doxxing content

**3. Flag only reviews that actually breach Trustpilot rules.**  
Trustpilot allows businesses to flag reviews that breach guidelines, including fake reviews, harmful content, and personal information, but misuse of flagging tools is itself a risk. ([legal.trustpilot.com](https://legal.trustpilot.com/for-reviewers/guidelines-for-businesses?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

**4. Do not mass-flag all negative reviews.**  
That will look defensive and can backfire.

**5. Respond to genuine negative reviews with a three-part structure.**
- acknowledge concern
- say you are investigating/taking action
- move to an offline channel

#### Example Trustpilot response
> We are very sorry for the concern this incident has caused. We are cooperating fully with the ongoing environmental health investigation and have taken immediate precautionary action. If you visited the restaurant and would like support, please contact [team/email] with your booking details so we can assist directly.

### What not to do
- do not buy or solicit fake balancing reviews
- do not pressure staff/friends to post reviews
- do not selectively suppress legitimate reviews

The CMA’s 2025 fake reviews guidance says businesses that publish consumer reviews must take steps to prevent fake reviews and misleading review information. ([gov.uk](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/fake-reviews-cma208?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

### Recovery objective
The goal is not “erase criticism.” It is:
- remove clear fakes under platform rules
- answer genuine complaints fast
- increase authentic positive review volume over time
- fix root causes so future reviews improve

That is exactly the pattern seen in [CS04] [CS04].
